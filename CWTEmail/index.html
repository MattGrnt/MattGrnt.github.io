Latest Cancer Waiting Times Stats and Policy Commentary

Here are the headlines from today’s data releases covering waiting times for cancer diagnosis and treatment in England in July 2024. From October 2023, NHS England has implemented changes to Caner Waiting Times standards. These include:

  • Retiring the Two Week Wait (TWW) standard to focus on the Faster Diagnosis Standard (FDS) (see further details below)

  • Combining first and subsequent treatments for the 31-day standard (previously only first treatment)

  • Combining the source of the referral for the 62-day standard (previously only Urgent Suspect Cancer, now also including Breast Symptomatic, Screening and Consultant Upgrade)

  • Providing a more granular tumour type breakdown for a range of cancers for both 31-day and 62-day standards.

Further details on the changes can be found on the NHS E website

Policy commentary

Cancer waiting times out today continue to show that services are struggling. In September 2024:

  • The 31-day target of 96% was not met with only 90.6% of patients starting cancer treatment within 31 days of a decision to treat.
  • The 62-day target of 85% was not met with only 67.3% of patients starting treatment for cancer within 62 days of a referral. This meant that around 8,600 people who started treatment for cancer in September waited longer than 62 days following a referral.
  • September 2024 saw around 1,800 (11.4%) patients waiting more than 104 days to begin treatment for cancer following an urgent suspected cancer referral.
  • The Faster Diagnosis Standard target of 75% was not met with only 74.8% of people diagnosed or had cancer ruled out within 28 days of an urgent suspected cancer referral.

Diagnostic tests waiting times

Target: <1% patients waiting ≥6 weeks

In September, more than 1,190,000 people were waiting for key diagnostic tests in England. Of these 18.3% have been waiting six weeks or more. Endoscopy waiting lists had 26.6% (around 43,400) of people waiting six or more weeks, while radiology waiting lists had 17% (more than 174,000) waiting six or more weeks.

Note, diagnostic tests waiting times data now include barium studies as a key diagnostic test.

Faster Diagnosis Standard performance

Note

Target: 75%, MISSED at 74.8%

74.8% of people were diagnosed or had cancer ruled out within 28 days of an urgent suspected cancer referral in September 2024, missing the NHS E target of 75%. If the target was met, around 410 additional patients would have been given a cancer diagnosis or had cancer ruled out on time in September 2024.

One month (31-day) wait from decision to treat to starting treatment for cancer

Target 96%, MISSED at 90.6%

Only 90.6% of patients started treatment within 31 days of a decision to treat in September 2024, missing the NHS E target of 96%. The target has not been met since December 2020.
If the target was met, around 2,900 additional patients would have been treated on time in September 2024.

Two month (62-day) wait from referral to starting treatment for cancer

Target 85%, MISSED at 67.3%

Only 67.3% of patients started treatment within 62 days of a cancer referral in September 2024, missing the NHS E target of 96%. The target has not been met since December 2015 - If the target was met, around 4,700 additional patients would have been treated on time in September 2024. Around 11.4 of patients ( around 1,800 ) waited more than 104 days after an urgent suspected cancer referral to begin treatment for cancer in September 2024
